5 Powerful Words of Quran

Assalamu Aleikum, Friends and Family! Hope you all are well.

🇮🇳 हिन्दी English

You know, our Quran is like a treasure. Inside it Allah has kept a lot of beautiful gifts for us. But sometimes we feel that it is very difficult to understand it. We think that we should be a great scholar.

But it is not true! Today I will tell you about 5 special words of Quran. These words are very powerful. If we use them, our life can be very peaceful and happy. Our Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) has also told us about them.

Let's start.

1. Tawheed (Oneness of Allah)

What does it mean?

It means believing that Allah is one. There is no one like Him. He has no partner, no son, no daughter. He is our God. This is the first thing all the prophets taught.

Why is it important?

It makes you brave. You are not afraid of anyone because you know that only Allah is powerful. You can talk to Allah directly. There is no need for anyone in between.

When you believe in this, all your good deeds become worship. Even eating becomes worship when you say بِسْمِ اللَّهِ (Bismillah)!

How to use it?

Always remember that Allah is with you. Before doing anything, think, "I am doing this only for Allah."

2. Taqwa (Fear of Allah)

What does it mean?

It means loving Allah so much that you are afraid to displease Him. You avoid bad deeds because you know He is watching.

Why is it important?

Allah promises! He says that if you follow Taqwa, He will show you a way out of every difficulty. (Surah At-Talaq).

He will provide you sustenance from a place where you cannot even imagine.

It purifies your character and protects you from harm.

Your heart becomes pure and peaceful.

How to use it?

Before doing anything, just ask: "If Allah is watching me, will He like this?" If your answer is no, then leave it.

3. Sabr (Patience)

What does it mean?

Sabr does not mean only waiting. It means to remain calm when trouble comes, not to get angry, not to complain and to trust in Allah.

Why is it important?

Allah says that He is with those who are patient. What can be better than the company of Allah?

When you are in trouble and are patient, Allah gives you a great reward.

It makes you strong from within.

How to use it?

Patience is not easy, but it makes you very strong. When you go through a loss or difficulty and say, إِنَّا لِلَّٰهِ وَإِنَّا إِلَيْهِ رَاجِعُونَ (Inna lillahi wa inna ilayhi raji'un - We belong to Allah and will return to Him), Allah gives you a great reward.

4. Shukr (Thanks)

What does it mean?

To thank Allah for everything. For your eyes, for your food, for your family. To have a feeling of gratitude in your heart.

Why is it important?

Allah says that if you show gratitude, He will give you more. (Surah Ibrahim).

When you say Alhamdulillah, your heart becomes happy. You stop looking at what others have.

This makes Allah very happy with you.

How to use it?

Say الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ (Alhamdulillah) many times throughout the day. While eating, while waking up, while seeing something good. Be happy with what Allah has given you.

5. Rahmat (Mercy)

What does it mean?

To be kind and gentle towards others. To humans, to animals, to everyone. Forgive those who do wrong to you.

Why is it important?

Our Prophet (peace be upon him) said: "Allah will be merciful to those who are merciful to others." If you want Allah's mercy on the Day of Judgment, you have to be merciful to others in this world.

It softens your heart and removes hatred.

How to use it?

Be nice to your parents. Help your wife. Feed a hungry animal. Don't yell at anyone. Forgive small things and mistakes.

Last Words

My dear friends, understand? Quran is not complicated. Allah has given us simple rules for a happy life.

First of all, believe in one Allah (Tawheed). Then, always remember Him (Taqwa). When trouble comes, be patient (Sabr). Always be thankful for blessings (Shukr). And be kind to everyone (Rahmat).

This is a simple secret. May Allah help all of us to take advantage of these blessings. Amen.

Please share with your children and friends. If you liked it, do let me know your feedback.

Jazakallah Khair.
